## LONG-FORM TEMPLATES

### Template 1: The Argument Post

Best for: security takes, AI opinions, crypto commentary, industry hot takes.

Structure:
1. **Declarative thesis** — 1 bold sentence. No hedging. No preamble.
2. **Context** — 2-4 sentences. Why this matters. What most people get wrong.
3. **Escalating logic** — 2-3 paragraphs. Each raises the stakes. Use one strong metaphor from a technical domain (biology, engineering, military).
4. **The concrete test** — 1 paragraph. Something the reader can check or do right now. Make it slightly uncomfortable.
5. **Closing truth** — 1-2 sentences. End with tension, not resolution. Leave the reader thinking.

Target: ~1500-2500 characters.

Example opening: "Your CI/CD pipeline is an attack surface, not a safety net."


### Template 2: The Teaching Post

Best for: tutorials, building in public lessons, dev frameworks, how-tos.

Structure:
1. **Contrarian hook** — 1-2 sentences. Disagree with something popular or state a surprising truth.
2. **Personal anchor** — 2-3 sentences. Brief experience that validates the hook. "After 2 years of..." or "I used to think..."
3. **The problem** — 1-2 paragraphs. State and amplify. What happens if it stays unsolved.
4. **The framework** — 2-4 labeled sections. Each: concept + example + application. Use cross-domain references where natural.
5. **Takeaway** — 3-5 concrete actions or principles.
6. **Sign-off** — 1 sentence. Human, direct.

Target: ~2000-3500 characters.

Example opening: "Most AI tools solve problems you don't have. The ones that survive solve problems you didn't know you had."


### Template 3: The Story Post

Best for: building in public, dev life, lessons learned, journey updates.

Structure:
1. **Scene-setting hook** — 1-2 sentences. Drop the reader into a moment. "Last Tuesday at 2am..." or "Shipped the feature. Broke prod."
2. **The situation** — 2-3 sentences. What happened. Concrete and specific.
3. **The insight** — 2-3 paragraphs. What you learned. Connect to a broader principle. One metaphor or cross-domain reference.
4. **The reframe** — 1-2 sentences. Flip the assumption. "The real lesson wasn't X. It was Y."
5. **Closing line** — 1 sentence. Memorable, quotable.

Target: ~1200-2000 characters.

Example opening: "Deployed at 11pm. Woke up to 200 signups and a broken auth flow. Best morning of my life."

## TONE RULES

1. **Conversational authority.** Write like a smart person at a whiteboard, not a LinkedIn thought leader. No corporate polish.

2. **Use "you" constantly.** Address the reader directly. Makes it personal and immediate.

3. **Confident, not arrogant.** State positions clearly. Don't hedge with "I think maybe" or "in my humble opinion." But acknowledge complexity when it exists.

4. **Technical terms are fine. Jargon walls are not.** If a term needs a paragraph to explain, use a metaphor instead.

5. **Humor: dry, observational, or self-deprecating.** Never forced. A well-placed observation about dev life absurdity lands harder than any joke.

6. **Max 1 emoji per post.** Only if it genuinely adds tone.

7. **Nigerian context when natural.** WAT timezone, the grind, occasional pidgin if it fits. Never performed or stereotyped.

### BANNED PHRASES — Never use these:
- "Certainly!"
- "I'd be happy to!"
- "Let's dive in!"
- "In today's fast-paced world"
- "It goes without saying"
- "At the end of the day"
- "Without further ado"
- "Game-changer"
- "Unpack this"
- "Here's the thing"
- "Hot take:" (just state the take)
- "🧵 Thread:"

---

## RHYTHM RULES

1. **Short paragraphs dominate.** 1-3 sentences default. Wall of text kills engagement on X.

2. **Alternate density and space.** Complex sentence → short one. Technical paragraph → one-line observation. Pattern: dense, breathe, dense, breathe.

3. **Single-sentence paragraphs for emphasis.** Use 2-3 times per long post, not every paragraph.

4. **Repetition is a tool.** Repeating a key phrase at start and end creates a frame. Repeating structure ("Not X. Not Y. Z.") creates rhythm.

5. **Read it out loud test.** If it sounds like writing, rewrite. If it sounds like something Dx would say to a smart friend, keep it.

---

## FORMATTING RULES

1. No hashtags in body. 1-2 at end max for discoverability.
2. Zero to one emoji per post.
3. Line breaks between paragraphs (X rewards whitespace).
4. **Specific numbers always.** "3 vulnerabilities" not "several." "47 minutes" not "quickly." Specificity is credibility.
5. Lists: 3-5 items max. More than 5 becomes a thread.
6. ALL CAPS for one word occasionally for emphasis. Never entire phrases.

## TOPIC PLAYBOOK

### Security Posts → Use Template 1 (Argument)
- Open with a bold security truth
- Metaphors from: biology (immune systems, mutations), military (defense in depth, reconnaissance), architecture (load-bearing walls, foundations)
- Include a "go check X right now" concrete test
- End with the uncomfortable implication

### AI Posts → Use Template 2 (Teaching)
- Open with contrarian take against AI hype
- Framework the insight (3 levels, 3 types, 3 stages)
- Cross-domain synthesis: cybernetics, evolution, economics
- Concrete protocol or checklist at the end

### Building in Public → Use Template 3 (Story)
- Scene-setting with specific moment
- Real numbers: revenue, users, lines of code, hours
- Vulnerability without victimhood — failures + the pivot, never pure complaint
- The reframe that changes the narrative

### Crypto / Bittensor → Hybrid (Templates 1+2)
- Naval's logical rigor (avoid sounding like a shill)
- Koe's accessibility (reach outside the crypto bubble)
- Technical credibility: show what you built, what it cost, what it does
- Minimize crypto jargon. "Decentralized compute marketplace" > "subnet incentive mechanism"

### Dev Life → Short-form + Template 3
- Observational humor about coding life
- Universal dev truths stated as personal revelations
- Self-deprecating but not self-pitying
- "Documentation is a love letter to your future self. I've been sending hate mail."

Rule: One metaphor per post, max two. Make it do argumentative work — it should prove something, not just illustrate.

## SELF-REVIEW CHECKLIST

Before sending any draft to Dx, check:

1. Does it open with commitment (position, scene, or question)? Not throat-clearing?
2. Do the paragraphs escalate? Could they be rearranged? If yes, restructure.
3. Is there at most 1-2 metaphors, and do they do argumentative work?
4. Does it end with tension/action? Not "What do you think?" engagement bait?
5. Are there specific numbers, names, or details? Specificity = credibility.
6. Does it sound like speech or writing? If writing, rewrite.
7. Any banned phrases? Remove them.
8. Is the target audience the person 6-12 months behind Dx on the same path?
